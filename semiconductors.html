Research Overview

NTHU Semiconductor Lab

"Working hands-on at the epicenter of silicon technology at NTHU, I engage in advanced hardware analysis, as well as the design and characterization of nanoscale transistors and memory cells. My work bridges theoretical microelectronics and physical engineering execution."

Core Research Pillars

Transistor & Memory Design

Assisting in the layout, simulation, and evaluation of next-generation semiconductor devices, focusing on scaling limits and electrical performance metrics essential for modern computational architectures.

Equipment Validation & Calibration

Ensuring complex lab machinery and characterization equipment operate within strict tolerances. This rigorous maintenance is crucial to prevent data drift and ensure the integrity of high-precision experimental results.

Hardware & Device Troubleshooting

Systematically isolating variables and diagnosing physical or script-based failures during wafer testing, device characterization, and data acquisition cycles to minimize downtime.

Data & Process Analysis

Utilizing solid-state physics and microelectronics principles to analyze the electrical characteristics (such as I-V curves) of transistors and memory arrays to support broader research objectives and yield optimization.
